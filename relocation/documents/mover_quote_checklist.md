# Cross-Border Mover Quote Checklist
Use this with `../checklist.md` before booking a mover.

This document is for the current move shape:

- mostly boxes
- a few small pieces
- maybe one bed
- full-service packing/loading and inside delivery needed

Do not request quotes as if this is still a furniture-heavy full 2-bedroom move if that is no longer true.

## Current working quote set
- Start with: MiniMoves, Ship Smart, and Trans Canada Movers
- If you compare large-network backups, confirm whether they will directly handle the move or broker/subcontract it
- If you compare boxes-only freight options, treat them as a separate self-pack path, not the default full-service path

The goal is not to prove one brand is always best. The goal is to get written scope, pricing, and customs handling that match your actual move.

## Before you request quotes
- Finish the current keep / sell / donate decision as much as possible
- Count the likely number of boxes and identify the larger items you still expect to move
- Take photos or a short video of the remaining shipment
- Start the living BSF186 / BSF186A spreadsheet now so you have a realistic item list
- Separate items you may hand-carry or exclude entirely:
  - passports and key documents
  - laptops and critical work devices
  - employer-owned equipment
  - plants
  - jewelry and irreplaceable items
- Confirm the Burnaby building move-in rules you already know: elevator booking, COI requirements, move window, and deposit

## What to ask every mover
Ask for written answers to all of the following.

### Service scope
- Will you do full packing at origin?
- Will you do full loading and unloading?
- Is inside delivery to the Burnaby apartment included?
- Is unpacking included, optional, or excluded?
- If one bed is moving, is bed disassembly / reassembly included?

### Pricing
- Is the quote binding, not-to-exceed, or estimate-only?
- What shipment size, weight, or volume assumptions is the quote based on?
- Is there a minimum shipment size or minimum charge for small loads?
- Exactly what triggers a change order or repricing?
- What valuation / liability coverage is included, and what are the upgrade options?

### Handling and handoffs
- Are you the actual carrier for pickup and delivery, or will any leg be brokered / subcontracted?
- If there is a handoff, who is the destination carrier?
- Who should be contacted if the pickup crew or delivery crew claims the scope is different?

### Customs and documentation
- Do you regularly handle California → British Columbia household moves?
- How do you coordinate CBSA customs clearance for settler's effects and goods to follow?
- What shipment inventory will you create, and when will you give it to me?
- How should your inventory line up with my BSF186 / BSF186A spreadsheet?
- Do you exclude any item categories from cross-border household shipments?

### Building and delivery details
- Can you provide a certificate of insurance if my building requires it?
- Can you meet a weekday move-in window and elevator reservation?
- Do you need anything in advance from the Burnaby building manager?

### Timing
- What is the pickup window?
- What is the delivery window?
- Is there any storage-in-transit assumption built into the quote?
- What happens if the apartment is not ready on the first proposed delivery date?

## Red flags
- The company will not say whether the move is direct or brokered
- The company refuses to put scope or pricing assumptions in writing
- The company prices it like a full apartment move even after you explain it is mostly boxes
- The company is vague about CBSA coordination
- The company assumes plants or employer-owned equipment can just be loaded with the household goods
- The company cannot explain minimum charges for small shipments

## Decision rule
Choose the mover whose written quote:

- matches your actual downsized inventory
- clearly states who handles pickup and delivery
- works with your building and timing constraints
- gives you a defensible customs / inventory workflow
- does not rely on vague verbal promises

## Short comparison notes to capture
For each mover, record:

- company name
- rep name and contact
- quote date
- quote type: binding / not-to-exceed / estimate
- quoted amount
- minimum weight / volume
- direct carrier vs broker / subcontractor
- customs coordination notes
- inside delivery / unpacking included?
- COI / building paperwork support
- pickup window
- delivery window
- major exclusions
- why this quote is better or worse than the others
