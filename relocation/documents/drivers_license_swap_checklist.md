# California DMV → ICBC Driver's License Swap Checklist
Use this with `../checklist.md` before and after arrival.

This is the conservative, checklist-ready version:

- order the California proof early
- bring the current California licence plus that proof to ICBC
- apply within 90 days of becoming a B.C. resident
- do not overcomplicate the DMV step with unsupported assumptions about sealing, certification, or in-person-only handling unless the DMV or ICBC specifically tells you to

## What is officially supported
- ICBC gives new B.C. residents 90 days to switch to a B.C. driver's licence
- If you cannot prove more than 2 years of full, non-learner driving experience, ICBC can place you into the Graduated Licensing Program (GLP)
- ICBC accepts proof that shows the original issue date of your first full licence
- ICBC can credit up to 15 years of driving experience for insurance purposes

## Before leaving California
- Order California DMV proof of your original licensing date as early as possible
- Request a driver record and, if needed, a First Issue Date Letter / letter of experience
- Keep old expired California licences if they show an earlier issue date than the current card
- Save copies of everything you receive from the DMV

## What to bring to ICBC
- Current California driver's licence
- California DMV proof of original issue date / driving experience
- Passport or other accepted identity document
- Proof of B.C. address

## Timing
- Apply within 90 days of becoming a B.C. resident
- Do not treat Day 1 or Day 2 as a special legal deadline; the real official rule is the 90-day window
- Even so, order the California proof early so you are not waiting on DMV paperwork after arrival

## Important rule
- If you cannot prove at least 2 years of non-learner driving experience, ICBC can place you into the GLP

## Insurance experience follow-up
- ICBC may request additional California driving-history / insurance-history proof later if you want to claim up to 15 years of insurance experience credit
- Treat that as a possible second-step insurance-credit issue, separate from the first-step licence exchange itself

## Do not assume
- Do not assume the current California record automatically shows the original issue date ICBC wants
- Do not assume ICBC only needs the current licence card
- Do not assume a same-day in-person certified DMV result is always available
- Do not assume sealed-envelope or special certification handling is required unless the DMV or ICBC specifically says so

## Practical rule
- Get the California proof before the move if possible
- Bring it with the current California licence, identity document, and B.C. address proof
- Use the 90-day window, but do not leave the California paperwork step to chance
