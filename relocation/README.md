# relocation

Planning notes and supporting documents for a California → British Columbia move.

## Current working facts

- planned move from California to British Columbia in early June 2026
- no US employment after departure
- no California home retained after the move
- household move is intended to be a real relocation rather than a temporary absence
- regular taxable brokerage holdings include US stocks / ETFs bought with cash

These facts make the California break materially cleaner and support using a clear Canadian arrival-date valuation record for the taxable portfolio.

## Default brokerage / tax strategy

Use this as the current working rule set:

1. sell loss positions before leaving the US if you want those losses recognized in the US / California system
2. hold gain positions through the move
3. establish Canadian residence clearly in June 2026 and break California ties quickly
4. update the primary US brokerage truthfully after the move; do not keep a false US address
5. record fair market value for each holding on the Canadian arrival date, together with the CAD/USD rate used
6. transfer in kind to a Canadian-friendly USD brokerage only if broker policy makes that necessary
7. target gain sales in early 2027 rather than during the 2026 transition year

One-line rule: losses before move, gains after move, full honesty with financial institutions, and meticulous June 2026 valuation records.

## Why early 2027 is the default sale window

- 2026 is a transition year with US federal, California, and Canadian part-year filing complexity
- early 2027 is generally the cleaner point to realize gains after a well-documented move
- the key goal is to make the California exit real on paper and avoid drifting back into US tax residency tests

Late-2026 gain sales may be more defensible with these facts than in a weaker fact pattern, but they are still not the default plan.

## Evidence to preserve

Keep a dated folder with:

- proof US employment ended at or before departure
- proof no California home was retained
- lease, border entry, mover receipts, and other records showing Canada became the center of daily life
- brokerage statements, lot-level basis reports, and pre-move holdings screenshots
- Canada arrival-date holdings quantity and fair market value by ticker
- CAD/USD exchange-rate support used for the Canadian basis record
- day-count support for any US presence in late 2026 and 2027

## Houseplants and the move to Canada

Use a conservative rule for plants:

- declare every plant
- check admissibility in CFIA's AIRS before travel
- do not assume a normal houseplant is automatically fine when packed into household goods

For this move, the practical rule is:

- hand-carry maybe
- movers shipment no, unless the exact plant and shipment method are clearly supported by AIRS or CFIA

### Why the guidance is conservative

- plant rules depend on exact species, origin, destination, and whether the plant has soil or growing media attached
- soil is a major risk factor; even small amounts can make a plant count as "with soil"
- indoor houseplants are stronger candidates than outdoor or patio-kept plants
- a movers shipment is operationally riskier than a clearly declared plant travelling with you

### Best candidates vs weak candidates

Better candidates:

- indoor pothos (`Epipremnum aureum`; older CFIA examples may refer to pothos as `Scindapsus` spp.)
- indoor snake plant (`Dracaena trifasciata`, also commonly labeled `Sansevieria`)
- other clearly identified indoor-only houseplants after AIRS verification

Weak candidates:

- outdoor or patio-kept plants
- plants with uncertain species identification
- large potted plants being buried inside a movers shipment without specific clearance

### Practical decision rule

If a plant is important enough to try to save, confirm all of the following first:

- exact scientific name
- indoor-only versus outdoor/patio history
- whether the plant will be hand-carried or shipped with movers
- whether soil or growing media is allowed for that exact case
- whether a permit, phytosanitary certificate, or inspection is required

If those answers are not clear, the safer decision is not to ship the plant.

### Question to send CFIA if needed

Use a short species-specific question that asks for:

- admissibility from the continental U.S. into British Columbia
- hand-carry versus household-goods shipment with movers
- whether soil / growing media is allowed
- whether any permit, phytosanitary certificate, or inspection is required
